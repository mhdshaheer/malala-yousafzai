About Malala

Malala Yousafzai is a Pakistani activist for female education and the youngest Nobel Prize laureate. She is known for human rights advocacy, especially the education of women and children in her native Swat Valley in northwest Pakistan, where the local Taliban had at times banned girls from attending school.

Born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Pakistan, Malala gained global attention after she was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman in 2012 as she was returning home from school on a bus. She survived the attack and has continued her advocacy for education rights.

Her courage and resilience in the face of extremism has inspired millions around the world. Today, through her Malala Fund, she continues to champion every girl's right to 12 years of free, safe, quality education.

Malala's Journey

2008-2009

Speaking Out Against Taliban

At just 11 years old, Malala began blogging for the BBC about living under Taliban rule using the pseudonym "Gul Makai." She became an outspoken advocate for girls' education.

October 9, 2012

Assassination Attempt

While returning home from school, Malala was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman. The assassination attempt sparked global outrage and support for her cause.

2013

Recovery and Return to Advocacy

After multiple surgeries and rehabilitation in the UK, Malala returned to her advocacy work. She addressed the United Nations on her 16th birthday, calling for worldwide access to education.

2014

Nobel Peace Prize

At age 17, Malala became the youngest-ever Nobel Prize laureate, receiving the Nobel Peace Prize jointly with Kailash Satyarthi for their struggle against the suppression of children and young people.

2017

Accepted to Oxford University

Malala was accepted to study Philosophy, Politics, and Economics at the prestigious Oxford University in the United Kingdom.

2020-Present

Global Advocacy Continues

After graduating from Oxford, Malala continues her global advocacy through the Malala Fund and other platforms, working to ensure girls everywhere have access to education.

The Malala Fund

Founded in 2013, the Malala Fund invests in education programs to help girls go to school and reach their full potential. The organization works in regions where girls face the greatest barriers to education.

Honorary Canadian Citizenship

In 2017, Malala received honorary Canadian citizenship, becoming the youngest person and only the sixth person in history to receive this honor.

Oxford University Graduate

Malala graduated from Oxford University in 2020 with a degree in Philosophy, Politics, and Economics, fulfilling a personal goal while continuing her global advocacy work.

International Children's Peace Prize

In 2013, Malala was awarded the International Children's Peace Prize for her courageous stand against the Taliban's efforts to deny girls the right to education.
